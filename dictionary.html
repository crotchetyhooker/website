Dictionary

C

constipation

[kahn-stip-ay-shun]

noun

  1. A state in which you painfully realize the lack of fiber in your diet The urge to buy yarn.​

E

errata

[ee-rah-tuh]

noun

  1. Mistakes found in a pattern that come at the most inconvenient time.​
  2. A staple of every pattern in existence

F

frogging

[frahg-eeng]

verb

  1. The act of crocheting with a yarn, then ripping out your work.​
  2. noun 'frogged': refers to now ruined yarn from overuse. Sometimes washing it fixes it, sometimes you're screwed.

G

gauge swatch

[gay-jj swatch]

noun

  1. In theory, a small crocheted sqaure that will help you avoid making something the wrong size.
  2. In reality, a goddamn waste of time.

H

hooker

[huk-ur]

noun

  1. One who crochets or 'hooks'. On the other hand, I'd do a lot for merino wool.
  2. Origin: crochet is derived from the French word for 'hook' which is where the tool's name comes from- not for our habit of offering sexual favors for yarn.
  3. On the other hand, I'd do a lot for merino wool.

M

magic Ring

[maj-ick reeng]

noun

  1. A magical ring of yarn that allows you to do things in the round without having a black hole in the center.
  2. Not as easy as ch3.

N

needle Junkie

[need-ull juh-n-kee ]

noun

  1. One who knits. This is typically done with two needles or more. Said needles can have one or two pointy ends, be joined by plastic cord or be thick enough for light saber duels.

S

skein

[skayn]

noun

  1. A very flexible term that refers to one bundle of yarn. Rhymes with 'cocaine'. Possibly not a coincidence.

stash

[stash]

noun

  1. Refers to the hoarding problem innate in a yarniac. We will use all of it one day. For sure. Maybe. Possibly. ​

stitch rage

[st-itch wr-aage]

noun

  1. The state that occurs when you must rip stitches out and are forced to deal with frogged yarn.
  2. verb: “stitch raging”

Y

yarniac

[yarn-ee-ack]

noun

  1. Broader term applying to those who use yarn for crafting.
  2. i.e; you, me and everyone else on Ravelry.

yarning

[yarn-eeng]

verb

  1. To be doing something with yarn. This can be knitting, crocheting, plastic canvas, weaving, spinning or dyeing.
  2. Nota bene: This does not include using yarn on those stupid cheap plastic looms. Stop cheating.

yarn-on

[yarn on]

noun

  1. That excited feeling you get when you walk into yarn shops. Or when you touch baby alpaca yarn. Or when someone buys you yarn. Or when you walk into a swim in a huge stash of yarn.
